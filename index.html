About me

I'm an undergraduate student at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering, with a strong foundation in both theoretical and applied aspects of computing. With a GPA of 4.8/5.0 and coursework in machine learning, algorithms, optimization, and data science, I thrive at the intersection of technical rigor and real-world impact.

My experiences range from machine learning research at the MIT Energy Initiative to developing quantum-based image processing methods at Arizona State University. I’ve also led hands-on projects like MIT Pokerbots, where I applied strategic modeling and algorithms to competitive gameplay.

Beyond research, I’ve served as a lab assistant, helping new students navigate programming challenges, and worked in industry as an associate consultant, contributing to database systems and vendor communication. I actively engage in professional development through groups like The CRWN and the MIT Undergraduate Practice Opportunities Program.

Passionate about building accessible, data-driven solutions, I’m always looking to collaborate on impactful tech and research projects.

Projects & Work

Distributed Energy Resource Dataset in California

Developing DER and socioeconomic datasets to simulate realistic distributed energy resource (DER)-rich grid environments. I applied statistical and machine learning methods such as linear, LASSO, Random Forest, and ridge regression to predict DER adoption based on socioeconomic factors at the county level. I also applied dimensionality reduction techniques such as PCA, tSNE, and UMAP to aid in clustering of counties.

Inspirit AI Presentation

Won first place at Inspirit AI by presenting a machine learning analysis on racial bias in the COMPAS criminal justice algorithm. Used logistic regression, Bernoulli NB, and Random Forest models to highlight disparities in sentencing predictions.

MIT PokerBots

Built a poker-playing bot in Python for MIT’s competitive PokerBots tournament. Engineered strategy adjustments under uncertainty and performed opponent modeling. Our team ranked in the top third out of all submissions.

Quantum Image Processing

Interned at ASU’s SenSIP Lab to develop a quantum-enhanced image processing pipeline using a hybrid 2D Quantum Fourier Transform. Researched the performance of quantum methods versus classical techniques for efficiency in image analysis.

Ratel Consulting

Supporting internal operations by building a user-friendly database with SQL, Excel, and Power BI. Streamlining workflows and enabling better data visibility for consulting teams working on strategic analyses.

Girls Who Code Projects

Created multiple web apps including a portfolio, personality quiz, and environmental awareness site using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. Gained foundational coding experience and published projects in the Girls Who Code catalog.
