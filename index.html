About Me

About Me

I’m an undergraduate at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), studying Computer Science and Engineering. I’m especially interested in how data, energy systems, and cities intersect—and how we can use technology to build fairer, more resilient infrastructure. Across my work, I like taking messy, real-world problems and turning them into clean, interpretable tools or insights. I’ve built large-scale datasets on distributed energy resources in California, designed models to understand where technologies like rooftop solar and EV charging are (and aren’t) being adopted, and explored how those patterns connect to equity and policy. On the research side, I’ve worked with * **MIT Energy Initiative (MITEI)** on data-driven energy and climate questions * **Arizona State University’s SenSIP Lab** on quantum-enhanced image processing I enjoy projects where I get to move between modeling, implementation, and storytelling—whether that’s writing Python pipelines, visualizing results, or presenting to non-technical audiences. Outside of research, I’ve been a lab assistant for MIT’s introductory CS course, helping new programmers debug code and build confidence. I’ve also worked in industry as an associate consultant, supporting database design, internal tooling, and communication with external partners. When I’m not in front of a terminal, you can usually find me dancing, tinkering with side projects, or planning the next thing I want to learn. I’m always excited to collaborate on projects at the intersection of **data, energy, and urban systems**—especially when there’s a clear path from technical work to real-world impact.

Projects & Work

Distributed Energy Resource Dataset in California

Developing DER and socioeconomic datasets. Applying statistical and machine learning methods such as linear, LASSO, Random Forest, and ridge regression to predict DER adoption based on socioeconomic factors at the county level. Also used dimensionality reduction techniques such as PCA, t-SNE, and UMAP to aid in clustering of counties with K-Means.

Inspirit AI Presentation

Won first place at Inspirit AI by presenting a machine learning analysis on racial bias in the COMPAS criminal justice algorithm. Used logistic regression, Bernoulli NB, and Random Forest models to highlight disparities in sentencing predictions.

MIT PokerBots

Built a poker-playing bot in Python for MIT’s competitive PokerBots tournament. Engineered strategy adjustments under uncertainty and performed opponent modeling. Our team ranked in the top third out of all submissions.

Quantum Image Processing

Interned at ASU’s SenSIP Lab to develop a quantum-enhanced image processing pipeline using a hybrid 2D Quantum Fourier Transform. Researched the performance of quantum methods versus classical techniques for efficiency in image analysis.

Ratel Consulting

Supported internal operations by building a user-friendly database with SQL, Excel, and Power BI. Streamlined workflows and enabled better data visibility for consulting teams working on strategic analyses.

Girls Who Code Projects

Created multiple web apps including a portfolio, personality quiz, and environmental awareness site using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python. Gained foundational coding experience and published projects in the Girls Who Code catalog.
